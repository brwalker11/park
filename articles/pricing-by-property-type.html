Office Buildings: The Weekday Commuter Model

Demand pattern: High weekday mornings, moderate all day, low evenings and weekends

Strategy

  • Morning rush (7-9 AM): $10-12/hour
  • Business hours (9 AM-5 PM): $6-8/hour
  • Evening (5-7 PM): $8-10/hour
  • After hours (7 PM-7 AM): $3-5/hour
  • Weekend flat rate: $15/day

The key: Offer monthly permits at $200-250/month for regular commuters. This gives them certainty while you capture dynamic rates from visitors and occasional parkers.

What works: Time-based pricing. Your demand is predictable.

Retail Centers: The Weekend Shopper Model

Demand pattern: Moderate weekdays, high weekend afternoons, holiday spikes

Strategy

  • First 2 hours: Free with purchase validation
  • 2-4 hours: $5
  • 4-6 hours: $12
  • 6+ hours: $20
  • Holiday season: +50% on all rates

The key: Partner with retailers to offer parking validation. First 2-3 hours free if they shop. Captures revenue from non-shoppers and extended stays.

What works: Hybrid approach. Time-based for weekday/weekend differences, plus event-based surges during holidays.

Hotels: The Guest vs. Public Model

Demand pattern: Varies with hotel occupancy, local events, tourist seasons

Strategy

  • Hotel guests: Fixed $25/night
  • Public hourly (off-peak): $8/hour
  • Public hourly (high-demand): $15-20/hour
  • Public daily max: $30-40/day
  • Monthly commuter permits: $150-200/month

The key: Keep guest rates simple and predictable. Use dynamic pricing only for public parking when you have excess capacity.

What works: Demand-based for public parking.

Event Venues: The Surge Model

Demand pattern: Extreme spikes during events, nearly empty otherwise

Strategy

  • Pre-booked (7+ days): $30
  • Pre-booked (1-6 days): $40
  • Day-of-event online: $50
  • At the gate: $60-75
  • VIP/premium lots: $100+
  • Non-event days: $10/day

The key: Capture maximum value during events through surge pricing, but encourage pre-booking with discounts.

What works: Event-based pricing with tiered rates based on when customers book.

Airport Parking: The Duration Model

Demand pattern: Consistent with seasonal spikes during holidays and summer

Strategy

  • Economy lot: $12-18/day (dynamic)
  • Standard lot: $18-25/day (dynamic)
  • Premium covered: $25-35/day (dynamic)
  • Valet service: Fixed $45/day
  • Hourly rate: $5-8/hour (first 24 hours)

The key: Offer tiered product options (economy, standard, premium) with dynamic pricing within each tier based on occupancy.

What works: Demand-based within product tiers, plus seasonal adjustments for holidays.

Multi-Family Residential: The Resident vs. Guest Model

Demand pattern: Mostly stable with resident parking, variable guest parking

Strategy

  • Resident parking: Included or fixed monthly ($50-100)
  • Guest hourly: $3-5/hour
  • Guest daily max: $15-20/day
  • Visitor overnight: $25/night
  • Non-resident monthly: $150-200/month

The key: Protect resident parking while monetizing excess capacity through guest parking.

What works: Fixed rates for residents, time-based dynamic pricing for guests.

Healthcare Facilities: The Patient-First Model

Demand pattern: High during business hours, especially mornings for appointments

Strategy

  • Patients: First 3 hours free with validation
  • Extended patient parking: $2-3/hour after free period
  • Visitor daily max: $15-20
  • Staff: Free with permit
  • Non-affiliated public: Premium rates

The key: Don't make sick people or worried families feel gouged. Keep patient parking affordable while capturing revenue from non-medical visitors.

What works: Validation system with generous free periods, time-based pricing for extended stays.

Finding Your Model

Look at your three busiest days last month. What do they have in common? That's your demand pattern. Look at your slowest days. What's different?

Your pricing strategy should amplify revenue during high demand and attract customers during low demand. Start conservative, measure results, and adjust.
